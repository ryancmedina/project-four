Can We Predict If an Asteroid Is Potentially Hazardous?

Yes — machine learning can help assess asteroid risk.

By analyzing features like diameter, velocity, miss distance, and orbit classification, we can predict whether an asteroid poses a danger to Earth.

Models such as Logistic Regression or Random Forests are trained on past asteroid data to recognize patterns that distinguish hazardous objects.

This allows scientists to quickly evaluate new threats and strengthen planetary defense — giving us valuable time to respond if needed.

What Are the Chances of a Hazardous Asteroid Hitting Earth?

Although the term “potentially hazardous asteroid” may sound concerning, the actual risk of one hitting Earth is extremely low. These objects are classified based on their size and proximity — not because a collision is expected.

In fact, no known asteroid poses a threat within the next 100 years. The estimated chance of a major impact this century is less than 0.03%.

NASA and international space agencies continuously monitor near-Earth space to detect any risks early and ensure we are prepared to respond if needed.
